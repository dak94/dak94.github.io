This project was also completed during my masters. It's a hypothetical/proposed identity for Edinburgh Deaf Festival, an event aimed at celebrating Deaf culture and helping a thoroughly disenfranchised community integrate in society by giving them a public presence that has been missing from our culture.

Contextualisation of promotional material made in Photoshop. This always makes the project seem more complete and it's also great to get out and about in the city using a camera when most of the production is digital.

I used waveforms as a motif in this project because it's a visualisation of sound, another way to use or consume sound without actually hearing it. This is intended as a visual metaphor for the workarounds Deaf people have for navigating a world where sound seems like an integral part of life.

I've always loved making typograms and I'll take any opportunity to do so, luckily this project offered a good reason to. Production of the wave was done in Touchdesigner but a very similar effect can be achieved in Adobe software albeit with a slightly lesser degree of control.
