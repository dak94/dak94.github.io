    Banished is an Opera about a group of 18th century Irish women aboard a prison ship bound for Australia.

    The brief was given to our college as a live brief for students to try. Deliverables for the brief were promotional content including a poster and the programme for the event.

    Inspired by nautical maps, the lines and concentric circles in this project are used and abused to create a sense of disappearing into the ocean.

    In black, white and red, the programme continues the motif of concentric circles and lines seen in the other pieces of promotional work and ephemera.

    The red dots in the poster represent the women aboard the ship, while the circles radiating out around them show interaction between them and their overall shared experience of being lost through visually representing the features of a nautical map.
